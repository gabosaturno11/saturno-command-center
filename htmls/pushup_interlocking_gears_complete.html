The So-Called Basics

Technique Before Reps
Master the correct, standardized technique for each basic movement. This allows for accurate self-assessment and ensures that progress is built on a solid, effective, and safe foundation, rather than reinforcing poor movement patterns.
Key Movement Cues
"Push the ground away" - Primary cue for scapular protraction and creating stability. "Tuck the pelvis in" - Maintain core tension. "Eye of the bicep forward" - Proper external shoulder rotation.
Find Your Progression
It is crucial to find the right progression for your current strength level. Whether using a high incline for push-ups, the goal is to select a variation that allows for 7-15 controlled repetitions, creating a clear starting point for long-term development.
Foundation First
You cannot jump to skills like the planche or front lever without first mastering your push-ups, dips, pull-ups, and rows. Building a strong foundation in the basics is the most critical and non-negotiable part of the journey.
Sensation Over Intensity
A core principle that dictates the how of progression selection. Focus on quality of movement and proper muscle activation rather than forcing through repetitions with poor form.
