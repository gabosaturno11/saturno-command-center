Core Philosophy & Mindset

The foundational worldview that informs every aspect of practice and instruction.

Calisthenics as Path to Self-Mastery

  • Movement as Universal Language: Return to our original language of movement
  • Growth Through Challenge: True progress forged in discomfort
  • Non-Dual Perspective: Art and science, discipline and play integrated
  • Authenticity and Vulnerability: Personal path that demands presence
  • Suffering as Framing: Obstacles as opportunities for deeper awareness
2

General Training Principles

Overarching rules and methods that guide systematic development.

  • Progressive Overload: Systematic increases through leverage, reps, tempo, complexity
  • Consistency Over Intensity: Regular imperfect practice beats sporadic perfection
  • Recovery & Longevity: Sustainable progress through stress-recovery cycles
  • Quality Over Quantity: Impeccable form and mindful execution priority
  • Direction Over Speed: Strategic movement yields compounding results
3

Specific Exercise Cues & Breakdowns

Technical instruction and analysis for fundamental movements.

Handstand

  • Setup: Wrists under shoulders, fingers spread
  • Cues: Push tall, ribs tucked, glutes engaged
  • Faults: Banana back, bent elbows, forward gaze

Planche

  • Setup: Protracted shoulders, locked elbows
  • Cues: Push floor away, compress core
  • Faults: Hips high, scapular collapse

Progression & Regression Models

Systematic pathways for skill acquisition and adaptation.

If-Then Logic

  • If unable to perform standard push-up → regress to incline/knee push-ups
  • If movement too easy → progress via foot elevation, tempo, isometric holds

Skill Trees & Spiral Progression

  • Visual maps showing prerequisites and logical progressions
  • Mastery is non-linear: revisit fundamentals at increasing complexity
